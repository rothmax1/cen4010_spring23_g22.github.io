Environmental Engineering


General Information

Environmental engineering focuses on protecting and improving the natural environment by designing and implementing solutions to environmental problems. Environmental engineers work to solve issues related to air and water pollution, waste management, environmental sustainability, and ecosystem preservation.

  3. Water Resource Engineer
    4. A water resource engineer focuses on the planning, design, and protection of water resources, such as rivers, lakes, groundwater, and wetlands. They use their knowledge of hydrology, hydraulics, and environmental science to develop and implement systems that store, purify, and distribute water for human and industrial use. These engineers work on a variety of projects related to water supply, flood control, irrigation, and wastewater treatment. They also need to consider the ecological impact of their projects and ensure compliance with relevant regulations. Strong analytical, problem-solving, and communication skills are essential for success in this field.
