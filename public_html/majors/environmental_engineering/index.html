Environmental Engineering


What Is It?

Environmental engineering focuses on protecting and improving the natural environment by designing and implementing solutions to environmental problems. Environmental engineers work to solve issues related to air and water pollution, waste management, environmental sustainability, and ecosystem preservation.



What Can I Do With It?

Environmental Engineer
An environmental engineer applies their understanding of engineering, chemistry, biology, and environmental science to address environmental issues. They work on preventing, controlling, and solving environmental problems such as pollution, waste management, and resource conservation. Their tasks may include creating and implementing systems for water treatment, air pollution control, or hazardous waste management while keeping in mind social and economic factors and complying with environmental regulations. A water resource engineer focuses on the planning, design, and protection of water resources, such as rivers, lakes, groundwater, and wetlands. They use their knowledge of hydrology, hydraulics, and environmental science to develop and implement systems that store, purify, and distribute water for human and industrial use. These engineers work on a variety of projects related to water supply, flood control, irrigation, and wastewater treatment. They also need to consider the ecological impact of their projects and ensure compliance with relevant regulations.
